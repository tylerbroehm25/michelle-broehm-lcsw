About Michelle

Honest, warm support — with a bit of humor

If you've been feeling stuck, repeating patterns you can't seem to break, or just emotionally drained — you're in the right place. I've spent over a decade helping adults and older adults move through anxiety, ADHD, life transitions, and more.

My approach is grounded in psychodynamic and social learning theory, using evidence-based tools like ACT, CBT, and motivational interviewing to help you understand your values, your behavior patterns, and how to take action that actually aligns with who you want to be.

I believe therapy should feel real, not clinical. If you don't mind a bit of honesty and the occasional laugh, I'd love to connect for a free 15-minute consultation.

Legal & Privacy

Privacy policy & disclosures

Information collected

When you submit the consultation request form on this website, I collect your name, email address, phone number (if provided), and any information you voluntarily share in your message. This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry and schedule a consultation. It is never sold, shared with third parties for marketing purposes, or used for any purpose other than providing you with care or responding to your request.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

As a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information is collected about you, request deletion of your personal information, and opt out of the sale of personal information (note: this practice does not sell personal information). To exercise any of these rights, contact me directly at (213) 322-1150 or via the client portal.

HIPAA & notice of privacy practices

This practice is subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Your protected health information is handled with the strictest confidentiality. A full Notice of Privacy Practices will be provided to you prior to or at your first appointment. All telehealth sessions are conducted via HIPAA-compliant platforms.

Telehealth informed consent (California B&P Code §2290.5)

In accordance with California Business and Professions Code §2290.5, clients receiving telehealth services will be provided with a telehealth informed consent form prior to the first session. This form explains the nature of telehealth services, your rights as a client, the limitations of remote care, and your right to withdraw consent at any time. Telehealth services are available to clients physically located in California at the time of service.

Confidentiality & limits of confidentiality

Everything discussed in therapy is confidential. Exceptions exist as required by California law, including situations involving imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, suspected abuse or neglect of a child, elder, or dependent adult, or when disclosure is ordered by a court. These exceptions will be explained in full during your intake process.

Not a crisis service

This website and practice are not a crisis service. If you are experiencing a mental health emergency, please call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), call 911, or go to your nearest emergency room.

Cookies & website data

This website does not use tracking cookies or analytics software. No browsing data is collected or stored by this site.

Licensure

Michelle Broehm is licensed as a Clinical Social Worker in the State of California, License #LCSW92693. Licensed and authorized to provide psychotherapy services to clients physically located in California. You may verify licensure at the California Department of Consumer Affairs license lookup.
